Johan Cruyff Legacy

Cruyff joined Amsterdam's Ajax soccer club's youth development squad when he was 10 years old. He made his senior team debut at the age of 17. He was transferred to FC Barcelona in 1973 after helping Ajax win six league titles, four national cups, and three European Champion Clubs' Cups (now known as the UEFA [Union of European Football Associations] Champions League). Barcelona, captained by Cruyff, won the Spanish League championship in 1974 and finished second in 1976 and 1977.

Cruyff played for several American teams, including the Los Angeles Aztecs and the Washington Diplomats, from 1979 to 1980, and was named the North American Soccer League's Most Valuable Player in 1979. He returned to Ajax in 1981, but two years later, due to a contract dispute, he joined rival team Feyenoord. He won his ninth Dutch championship and his fifth national cup there.

Cruyff coached Ajax (1987) and Barcelona (1989) to European Cup Winners' Cups after retiring as a player in 1984. He led Barcelona to the European Champion Clubs' Cup and the UEFA Super Cup in 1992, but he was fired in 1996. In 2009, he returned to the bench to manage the Catalan national team, a position he held until 2013.
